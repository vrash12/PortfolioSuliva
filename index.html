Online Classes among Public Schools Students


Distance learning─ an outward proof that distinguishes who are the privileged students, and those who are not, which provokes rise to divided learning capacities and capabilities of students. Given that we are experiencing a health crisis, or what we know as the COVID-19 pandemic, academic institutions were required to adhere to modify and change the manner or conducting classes to prevent the widespread of the virus─ inclining towards either modular or taking advantage of the benefits of technology by conducting online classes through different platforms. Considering that distance learning needs to make use of technology, students are therefore asked to have gadgets to be able to attend to online class, in which not all students are privileged enough to have such. Hence, I am not in favor of online classes among students in public school since not all students can afford to have a gadget suitable for online classes, not all have the means and proper resources such as stable connection, and lastly, not entirely have conducive places in home in making them focus while having virtual classes.


I believe that online classes should not be conducted among public students for several reasons.


First, not all students can afford to have gadget suitable for online classes. Seeing that pandemic posed a huge impact not only on the education system but for the entire business sector as well, it robbed several Filipino parents of a reliable and stable source of income, pushing them to the brink of insolvency (Bautista, 2020) and the majority of students in public schools come from low-income households (Dollanganger, 2020). Distance learning should be deeply or well-reviewed and scrutinized since the status of individual students are not the same and those who are privileged enough are the ones who get to afford the gadgets and necessary devices needed whereas those families who struggle financially are already having a hard time in adjusting their finances for necessities, the more in getting these equipment in their hands.


Second, not all students have the means and proper resources such as stable internet connection. According to Kritz (2020), merely 26% of public schools are connected to the internet or have the capability to connect to the internet, and almost 5,000 public schools in rural, secluded, and remote areas lack access to electricity and a stable internet connection is vital in conducting distance learning.


Lastly, not entirely have conducive places in home in making them focus while having virtual classes. In light of students who are residing in congested homes, this can also be a contributing factor in the lack of favorable learning environments at home thus affecting the efficacy of online lectures (Joaquin, Biana, & Dacela, 2020).


To sum it all, conducting online classes among students in public schools should not be pursued since with the predicaments and hindrances mentioned, not only one student will be left behind, but most affected students. This, alone, should be considered and the relevant authorities should suggest pertinent solutions to adhere to the needs of the students, mostly in the public school.
